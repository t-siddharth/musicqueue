Saskatchewan Cities: Regina and Prince Albert

Regina

Population: 226,404 (2021)

Land Area: 178.81 km²

Regina is the capital city of Saskatchewan and its second-largest city. It serves as a cultural and commercial center for southern Saskatchewan, offering a rich history and diverse population.

As of 2021, Regina's population is 226,404, with a land area of 178.81 km². The city has experienced steady growth over the years, reflecting its economic and cultural significance in the region.

Prince Albert

Population: 37,756 (2021)

Land Area: 67.17 km²

Prince Albert, known as the "Gateway to the North," is the third-largest city in Saskatchewan. It is located near the center of the province and serves as a hub for northern communities.

In 2021, Prince Albert had a population of 37,756, covering a land area of 67.17 km². The city plays a vital role in connecting the northern and southern regions of Saskatchewan, offering various services and amenities to its residents and surrounding communities.
